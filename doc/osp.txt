The primary specialist who creates and implements communication boards and other AAC systems is a Speech-Language Pathologist (SLP), often with specialized training in Augmentative and Alternative Communication. 
They work alongside occupational therapists, rehabilitation engineers, and assistive technology specialists to develop tailored communication strategies. 


Role of the SLP: SLPs assess user needs and design both low-tech (e.g., printed picture boards) and high-tech (e.g., speech-generating devices) systems.
Other Team Members: Rehabilitation engineers and Assistive Technology (AT) consultants frequently assist in designing customized solutions.
Interdisciplinary Team: Specialists often work together to determine the most effective communication method for the individual.
