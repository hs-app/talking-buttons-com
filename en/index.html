Simple AAC boards that speak

Talking Buttons turns your Android phone or tablet, Chromebook, or desktop Chrome into a big-button picture communication board — for anyone who needs help speaking.

Get it on Google Play Add to Chrome
Screenshot area — add board images to docs/images/ when ready.

Who it’s for

  • People who are non-speaking or have difficulty speaking (e.g. stroke, aphasia, autism)
  • Caregivers and family members
  • Schools and therapists using Chromebooks
  • Hospitals and clinics needing bedside communication

Android app vs Chrome extension

Android

Portable, works offline. Full-screen mode helps avoid accidental exits — useful for children or users with motor challenges.

Chrome

Runs on Chromebook and desktop Chrome via the side panel — great for classrooms, shared PCs, and clinics.

Same boards everywhere: export and import using the Open Board Format (OBF/OBZ). Build on a computer, use on a tablet — or the other way around.

Key features

  • Customizable — large buttons, text, colors, and font sizes
  • Picture buttons — add images; text stays readable on any background
  • Text-to-speech — uses your device or browser voices, many languages
  • Voice recording — record short messages for each button when TTS isn’t enough
  • Layouts — presets and custom grids for your phrases
  • OBF/OBZ — share boards with therapists or back them up; compatible with other AAC tools that support the standard

Privacy

Your communication boards and recordings stay on your device or in your browser — not on our servers. Read the privacy summary.
