Buttons that speak for you

Talking Buttons helps people who have difficulty with speech. It is a solution in the field of augmentative and alternative communication (AAC) that turns an Android phone or tablet, a Chromebook, or the Chrome browser on a computer into an easy-to-use communication board. It is available as an Android app or as a Chrome browser extension.

Who it's for

  • People who have difficulty speaking due to stroke, aphasia, autism, or other causes
  • Their caregivers and family members
  • Medical professionals, including specialists (neuropsychologist, speech therapist)
  • Hospitals and clinics for communicating with patients

Key features

  • Customizable — button count and size, text, color, and font size
  • Picture buttons — each button can have its own image
  • Text-to-speech — choose a voice from those installed on the device or browser
  • Voice recording — short recorded phrases on buttons when text-to-speech isn't enough
  • Predefined boards — ready-made layouts provided as examples and a starting point you can adapt to your needs
  • OBF/OBZ — transfer boards between devices and back them up; compatible with other AAC apps that support the open format

Android app and Chrome extension

Android

Portable, works offline on phones and tablets. Full-screen mode prevents accidental exits. Great for children and patients with limited mobility.

Chrome

Runs in Chrome browser or on Chromebook. Great on a home computer or laptop, as well as on a shared computer in a classroom or clinic. Ideal for setting up button images.

Same AAC boards everywhere: export and import using the portable Open Board Format (OBF/OBZ). Build and customize a board on a computer, use it on a tablet — or the other way around.

Privacy

Your communication boards and recordings stay on your device or in your browser — not on our servers. Read the privacy summary.
